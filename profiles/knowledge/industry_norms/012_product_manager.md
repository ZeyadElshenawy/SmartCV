# Product Manager — Resume Conventions

PM resumes are hard because PMs influence rather than build. Strong PM resumes (a) name the specific decisions owned, (b) attach outcome metrics to launches led, (c) signal the seniority axis (APM / PM / Senior / Principal / GPM).

**Tool / methodology categories:**

- **Roadmapping / spec:** Productboard, Aha!, Linear, Jira, Notion / Confluence.
- **Analytics:** Amplitude, Mixpanel, Heap, PostHog, Pendo, Looker, Mode, SQL / dbt.
- **Experimentation:** Optimizely, LaunchDarkly, Statsig, Eppo.
- **Research:** UserTesting, Maze, Dovetail, Productboard insights, Intercom.
- **Methodologies:** JTBD, OKRs, RICE / ICE prioritization, North Star metric, opportunity-solution trees.

**Metrics:**
- **Launch outcomes:** revenue / ARR, conversion, retention (day-N), activation, NPS, churn.
- **Adoption:** % of target users in N days.
- **Business case:** $ projected vs. realized.
- **Operational:** # experiments shipped, % A/B tests that launched.
- **Org scope:** squad size, # PMs reporting (for GPMs).

**Strong bullet examples for PM:**

- "Led international-payments expansion (3 currencies, 12-week timeline, 6 engineers + 2 designers); shipped on schedule, added $1.4M ARR in 9 months."
- "Defined v2 onboarding from 22 user interviews + funnel analysis on 84K monthly signups; day-1 activation rose from 31% to 40% (Statsig A/B, p<0.01)."
- "Drove experimentation-velocity across growth: self-serve A/B playbook, mentored 4 PMs; team experiment cadence rose from 2.4 to 6.1 tests/quarter per PM."
- "Owned the API-product P&L ($4.2M ARR); cut churn from 18% to 9% via a re-priced enterprise tier; self-serve trial converted 11% to paid."
- "Authored Q3 roadmap (3 themes, 14 initiatives); presented to 80 stakeholders; secured exec sign-off in one review."

**PM-specific anti-patterns:**

- "Worked closely with engineering" — replace with collaboration artifact (specs authored, blockers removed).
- "Drove product strategy" — empty unless the strategy and outcome are named.
- Bullets sounding like engineers' bullets ("Built the search feature"). PMs ship through a team — use Defined / Scoped / Prioritized / Coordinated / Shipped.
- No metrics anywhere. PM resumes without outcome numbers default to the bottom of recruiter sorts.
- Listing every methodology as a skill ("Agile, Scrum, SAFe, LeSS, JTBD, OKRs...") is meaningless without evidence of application.

**Seniority calibration.**
- **Associate PM:** owned a feature or two, senior PM approves major decisions.
- **PM:** owned a product area, ran 2–3 squads in parallel.
- **Senior PM:** owned a multi-team initiative or P&L, mentored PMs.
- **Group / Principal PM:** owned a product line or platform, set long-range strategy.

The Summary should signal the level. "Led 3 launches" at PM level signals appropriate scope; at Principal PM level signals under-leveling.

## Concrete rule for SmartCV

For PM roles, surface tools across Roadmapping, Analytics, Experimentation, Research sub-groups. Use PM-specific verbs (Defined, Scoped, Shipped, Prioritized, Coordinated) — never use builder verbs (Built, Implemented, Coded). Every launch bullet must include a measured outcome (revenue, conversion, retention, NPS) AND scope (squad size, timeline, surfaces). Match seniority signals to the candidate's level — flag inflated language for junior candidates and under-leveled language for senior candidates.
