# CAR Method (Challenge–Action–Result)

CAR is a compression of STAR that drops the separate Task slot and folds it into the Challenge. The three parts:

1. **Challenge** — the problem or opportunity you faced.
2. **Action** — what you did about it.
3. **Result** — the measurable outcome.

CAR is preferred over STAR for resume bullets when the Situation and Task are essentially the same thing — common in engineering work where "the situation was a slow service" *is* the task ("make it fast"). Using CAR avoids redundancy.

**Template:**
`[Challenge clause], <Action verb> <object>, <Result with metric>.`

Or, with the Action leading:
`<Action verb> <object> [to address Challenge], <Result with metric>.`

**Examples:**

- "When the nightly ETL began missing its 6am SLA, profiled the join in the attribution job and rewrote it to use partition pruning, cutting runtime from 6h to 22min."
- "Faced with a 4-week release cadence that was bottlenecking the product team, automated the manual deploy steps in a GitHub Actions pipeline; release cadence shifted to bi-weekly with no incident-rate increase."
- "After a Q2 outage traced to expired TLS certs, built a Slack-alerting cron that warns 14 days before any cert expiry; zero cert-related outages in the following 9 months."

**CAR vs. STAR vs. X-Y-Z.**
- **STAR** — Situation context adds genuine signal.
- **CAR** — Situation and Task are the same (most engineering work).
- **X-Y-Z** — Action is the most interesting part; Challenge is implicit.

Typical engineering mix: 60% X-Y-Z, 30% CAR, 10% STAR. Sticking to one template across every bullet creates rhythmic repetition that reads as AI-generated.

**When the Challenge is too long.** Compress to one clause under 12 words. Long context paragraphs bury the verb.

**When you have no Result.** Omit the bullet. Resume bullets are about completed accomplishments, not in-progress work.

**Avoid generic Challenges.** "Faced with the need to improve performance" is empty. Specifics: "When the nightly ETL began missing its 6am SLA", "After three customer escalations about search latency".

## Concrete rule for SmartCV

Use the CAR template (`<short Challenge clause>, <Action verb> <object>, <Result with metric>`) for engineering bullets where the situation and task are the same thing — typically performance, reliability, or process-improvement bullets. Compress the Challenge to one clause under 12 words, name the specific problem (not "the need to improve X"), and always close with a measurable Result. Vary across CAR, STAR, and X-Y-Z within the same role to avoid rhythmic repetition.
