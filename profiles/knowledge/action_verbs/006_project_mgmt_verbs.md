# Project Management and Coordination Verbs

Project management verbs describe planning, scoping, scheduling, tracking, and delivering work. Apply to PMs, engineering managers, tech leads, and any IC owning the delivery of a multi-week effort.

**Planning and scoping:**
Defined, Drafted, Established, Estimated, Forecasted, Outlined, Planned, Prioritized, Scoped, Sequenced, Specified.

**Coordination and execution:**
Aligned, Arranged, Assigned, Consolidated, Coordinated, Delegated, Dispatched, Facilitated, Orchestrated, Organized, Scheduled, Synchronized.

**Tracking and reporting:**
Audited, Forecasted, Monitored, Reported, Reviewed, Tracked.

**Delivery:**
Closed, Completed, Delivered, Executed, Finalized, Launched, Released, Shipped, Wrapped.

**Risk and issue management:**
Anticipated, De-risked, Escalated, Identified (risks), Mitigated, Resolved, Triaged, Unblocked.

**Process improvement:**
Automated, Centralized, Codified, Formalized, Implemented (process), Standardized, Streamlined, Systematized.

**Verbs to avoid:**
- "Drove" by itself — vague unless paired with what was driven and to where.
- "Owned" — overused; specify what ownership meant (planned, scoped, decided).
- "Took the lead" — say Led or Coordinated.
- "Shepherded" — folksy; use Coordinated or Led.
- "Wore many hats" — cliché; list the actual hats as separate bullets.

**Pairing PM verbs with outcomes.**
PM bullets need both a project scope (people, budget, timeline) and a delivery outcome (shipped on time, hit metric, unblocked dependency):

- "Coordinated the iOS-to-Android port across 3 mobile engineers and 1 designer; shipped on the original 8-week timeline despite a mid-project scope increase."
- "Defined the API spec for the new pricing service and aligned 4 downstream consumer teams; cut integration-rework cycles from 6 PRs/team to 1."
- "Planned and ran the migration from monthly to bi-weekly releases across 5 product teams; deployment-related incidents dropped from 3/month to 1/month."
- "Estimated and shipped the GDPR-readiness initiative ($180K budget, 4-month timeline, 6-person team) on schedule and 12% under budget."

**PM-specific scale signals:**
- Team size, budget, timeline (always include at least two of the three).
- Stakeholder count: "across N teams", "with N partner orgs".
- Decision count: "removed N blockers", "made N priority calls".
- Outcome: shipped on time, hit metric target, unblocked downstream work.

**Engineering manager nuance.** EM bullets blend PM verbs (Planned, Coordinated) with people-management verbs (Mentored, Hired, Promoted). The classic mistake is to write only people-management bullets and miss the delivery-and-execution side, or vice versa. A senior EM resume needs both: 1–2 bullets on team growth/health, 2–3 on delivery and outcomes.

**Avoid corporate process buzzwords.** "Stakeholder management", "executive alignment", "cross-functional synergy" are all hollow when used as standalone phrases. Replace with the specific action: who you aligned, what decision was unblocked, what timeline was hit.

## Concrete rule for SmartCV

Use PM/coordination verbs (Coordinated, Defined, Planned, Delivered, Shipped) when the source CV documents project ownership or delivery. Always include at least two of: team size, budget, timeline. Always include a delivery outcome (on-time/early/late, metric hit, dependency unblocked). For engineering manager resumes, balance people-management bullets with delivery-and-outcome bullets — never write only one type.
