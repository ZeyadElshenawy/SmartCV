# Research, Analysis, and Investigation Verbs

Analysis bullets describe data work, research, evaluation, and investigation — central to data scientist, data engineer, ML engineer, QA, and analyst roles, but also useful for engineers describing root-cause investigations or A/B test work.

**Data analysis:**
Aggregated, Analyzed, Audited, Calculated, Classified, Compiled, Computed, Correlated, Derived, Examined, Explored, Forecasted, Identified, Interpreted, Investigated, Mapped, Measured, Modeled, Parsed, Profiled, Quantified, Surveyed, Tracked, Visualized.

**Research and discovery:**
Assessed, Collected, Critiqued, Diagnosed, Discovered, Evaluated, Explored, Extracted, Identified, Inspected, Interviewed, Investigated, Researched, Reviewed, Studied, Summarized, Surveyed, Synthesized, Validated.

**Hypothesis testing:**
A/B tested, Benchmarked, Compared, Designed (experiments), Evaluated, Hypothesized, Replicated, Tested, Validated.

**Reporting findings:**
Authored, Briefed, Communicated, Documented, Drafted, Presented, Published, Reported, Summarized, Visualized, Wrote.

**Verbs to avoid:**
- "Looked into" — too informal; use Investigated or Analyzed.
- "Studied" by itself — too vague; specify what was studied and what was concluded.
- "Worked with data" — say what you did with the data.
- "Crunched numbers" — informal cliché.

**Pairing analysis verbs with metrics.**
Analysis work needs concrete deliverables: dataset size, sample size, model accuracy, dollar impact, or decisions influenced.

- "Analyzed 18 months of churn data (2.4M user-events) and identified the 3 product-friction points driving 60% of cancellations."
- "A/B tested 4 onboarding variants on 12,000 new users; the winner lifted day-7 retention by 11 percentage points."
- "Benchmarked 5 candidate ranking models on the held-out 50K-query test set; the gradient-boosted variant won at +0.04 NDCG@10."
- "Profiled the data pipeline and identified a join in the attribution job that was scanning 100% of the table; rewrote it to use partition pruning and cut nightly runtime from 6h to 22min."

Each example pairs an analysis verb (Analyzed, A/B tested, Benchmarked, Profiled) with the dataset scope and the measured outcome.

**Common scale signals for analysis bullets:**
- Dataset: "N rows", "N events", "N users", "N requests/day".
- Sample / experiment: "N participants", "p < 0.05", "95% CI".
- Model performance: accuracy, F1, AUC, NDCG, RMSE — name the actual metric.
- Time saved / runtime improvement: "from Xh to Ymin", "Nx faster".
- Business impact: dollar lift, retention point change, conversion delta.

**Avoid double verbs.** "Analyzed and investigated" is redundant — pick the more specific one. Same for "Researched and explored", "Designed and built". One precise verb beats two generic ones.

## Concrete rule for SmartCV

Use analysis verbs (Analyzed, Investigated, Profiled, Benchmarked, A/B tested) for data science, data engineering, ML, and QA bullets, and for any engineering bullet describing a root-cause investigation. Always include the dataset scope (rows, events, users) AND the measured outcome (model metric, latency change, business impact). Avoid double-verb constructions ("analyzed and investigated") — pick the more specific verb.
