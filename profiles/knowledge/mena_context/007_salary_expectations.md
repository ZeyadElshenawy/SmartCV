# Salary Expectations on MENA CVs

Stating a salary expectation on the CV — or in the cover note — is materially more common in MENA than in the US or UK, where the dominant advice is to defer the conversation until offer stage. There is no public source mandating a salary line on a MENA CV, but it appears regularly in three contexts:

1. **Egyptian private-sector applications via Wuzzuf or direct email.** Many JDs explicitly ask "Expected Salary (EGP, monthly net)" as an application question. Candidates who skip the field are often filtered out automatically.
2. **Gulf roles for expatriate candidates.** Because total compensation in the Gulf is built from a base salary plus housing allowance, transport allowance, schooling allowance, annual flight, and end-of-service gratuity, employers want a single "all-in expected package" number to compare candidates against budget bands.
3. **Egyptian fresh-grad and intern postings.** Because pay bands are wide and informal, recruiters often ask for the candidate's number first to avoid wasting interview cycles on a misaligned candidate.

Currency conventions:

- **Egypt:** EGP, monthly, usually quoted **net** (after tax and social-insurance deduction).
- **Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman:** SAR / AED / QAR / KWD / BHD / OMR, monthly base + benefits broken out separately. Income tax does not apply on personal salary in most Gulf jurisdictions, so "net" and "gross" overlap.
- **Morocco, Tunisia, Algeria:** MAD / TND / DZD, monthly net.

Counter-pattern: multinational subsidiaries (Microsoft, P&G, Unilever, Vodafone) operating in MENA generally follow their global policy and do NOT want a salary expectation on the CV — it is collected via a separate application form field or HR phone screen.

## Concrete rule for SmartCV

Do NOT default to including a salary expectation on the CV. Only surface a salary line when (a) the candidate has explicitly entered an expected number in their profile AND (b) the target JD explicitly asks for "expected salary" or the target market is Egyptian local-employer (non-multinational). When salary is included, format it as **"Expected Salary: {amount} {currency} / month, net"** for Egypt and Maghreb, and **"Expected Package: {amount} {currency} / month (base + standard allowances)"** for the Gulf. Place the line in the Personal Information block, not in a free-text cover-note paragraph. For US, UK, EU, or multinational targets, omit the field entirely even if the candidate provided it.

## What public sources document

**Salary on the Wuzzuf structured profile is a documented ranking input.** Wuzzuf's own help center (via search excerpt, 2026-05-12) states that "adding a net salary will improve your job matching recommendation." This confirms the structured-profile salary field is not purely informational; it influences which jobs the platform surfaces and how the candidate is ranked against employer searches.

**2025–2026 Egypt CS / software-engineer salary data (monthly figures unless noted):**

- Wuzzuf's own posting for "Computer Science & Computer Engineering Fresh Graduates" (Heliopolis, Cairo) advertises **up to EGP 8,000/month** starting (Wuzzuf job posting, accessed 2026-05-12 via search excerpt).
- Glassdoor (2026): Junior Software Engineer in Cairo typical pay range **EGP 7,583 – EGP 17,063** annually (note: this annualised range is suspiciously low and likely a Glassdoor unit-conversion artefact; Glassdoor data for Egypt is patchy and should be treated as a directional lower bound).
- Whatisthesalary.com (2026): Fresh-graduate software engineer salary in Egypt **EGP 10,000 – 25,000 / month**; junior roles average **EGP 15,000 – 25,000 / month**.
- PayScale (2026): Junior Software Engineer in Egypt — typical range EGP 6,016 – 15,840 annually (same likely-annualised artefact as above).

**Wuzzuf's published salary analytics for Egypt** (`wuzzuf.net/careers/`, accessed 2026-05-12 via search excerpts):
- "Arabic speakers receive approximately 5,500 EGP on average for entry-level positions, while English speakers collect around 13,000 EGP" — i.e., language adds a ~2.4× multiplier on entry-level pay.
- "French speakers in Egypt can collect 27,000 EGP or beyond" in industries where Arabic speakers earn 5,500 EGP.
- Customer-service median (2025): **EGP 5,550** Arabic-only, **EGP 13,500** with English fluency.
- Entry-level call-center: **EGP 7,000 – 9,000 / month**.

**Net-vs-gross convention** is documented in Wuzzuf's own tax-calculator content: full-time employees deduct 11% social insurance, subtract the EGP 20,000 annual exemption, and apply progressive tax rates; freelancers pay a flat 10% income tax. This confirms the file's claim that Egyptian salaries are quoted **net** on Wuzzuf.

I could not find a public source documenting verbatim Egyptian-recruiter phrasing ("ابعتلي رقم"), cultural penalties for over-quoting, or USD-pegging behaviour after the 2022–2024 EGP devaluation.
