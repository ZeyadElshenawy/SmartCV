# CV vs Résumé Terminology in MENA

The default term across the Middle East and North Africa is **"CV"** (curriculum vitae), not "résumé". Wikipedia's overview of the document type notes: "In the EMEA and Asian countries, a curriculum vitae (CV) is used for similar purposes." The North-American distinction — where "résumé" denotes a short career summary and "CV" is reserved for longer academic dossiers — does not map onto MENA usage. In Egypt, Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and the wider Gulf, employers, recruiters, and job boards consistently say "CV" regardless of document length.

Wikipedia's Curriculum Vitae article also notes that "CV and resume are sometimes used interchangeably" in US and Canadian contexts, but the same article frames "CV" as the dominant British-English term. MENA hiring inherits the British convention because of post-colonial educational ties, the dominance of British-style recruitment agencies in the Gulf, and the use of English-language CVs across multinationals operating regionally.

Practical consequences:

- File names submitted to local employers are usually `Name_CV.pdf`, not `Name_Resume.pdf`.
- Job postings on LinkedIn, Wuzzuf, Bayt, and direct corporate sites in MENA typically request "CV" in the application instructions.
- Candidates targeting US-headquartered multinationals through their MENA offices may still see "Resume" in the JD; both terms refer to the same artifact for the candidate.
- Length expectations are MENA-style (1–3 pages with personal data) rather than the US 1-page strict cap, but the document is still called a CV regardless of length.

## Concrete rule for SmartCV

When generating documents for a candidate whose target market is in the MENA region (Egypt, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, Oman, Jordan, Lebanon, Morocco, Tunisia, Algeria), default to the term **"CV"** in document titles, file names, and any user-facing labels. Default file name: `{LastName}_{FirstName}_CV.pdf`. Only switch to "Resume" if the job description explicitly uses that word or names a US-headquartered employer that uses American conventions in its MENA postings.

## What public sources document

Bayt.com's own page is titled "**Post Your CV** Online for Free" and the platform's help center, employer search demo, and blog all use the word "CV" exclusively — never "Resume" — for the document candidates upload (Bayt employer pages, accessed 2026-05-12). Bayt's CV-search-for-employers product also uses "CV" in its name. This is consistent with the file's headline rule.

A community redesign analysis of Wuzzuf's profile pages (Sabri 2018, abdusabri.com) refers to the document upload as "**Resumes**", suggesting Wuzzuf's internal label may use the English "Resume" in places — but I was **unable to fetch wuzzuf.net or help.wuzzuf.net directly** (HTTP 403 from this client and from the Wayback proxy I tried), so I cannot confirm whether the current candidate-facing UI says "CV" or "Resume". This contradicts the confident claim in earlier drafts of this section. The Wuzzuf careers blog (`wuzzuf.net/careers/...`) consistently uses "CV" in its salary and labor-law articles when referring to job-seeker documents.

External MENA-focused CV-advice sites (visualcv.com, kudoswall.com, robertwalters.ae) all use "CV" rather than "Resume" when writing about Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Gulf hiring (accessed 2026-05-12).
