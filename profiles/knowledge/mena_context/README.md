# MENA Hiring Context — Author's Original Research

This folder is intentionally empty except for this placeholder. It is reserved for the SmartCV author's (Zeyad's) **original research** on hiring norms in the Middle East and North Africa region — material that does not exist in any English-language resume guide and that does not get reliably surfaced by general-purpose LLMs.

The other six categories under `profiles/knowledge/` were drafted from public Western sources by the cowork session on 2026-05-12. This category is the **defensible original contribution** to the graduation-defense story — it cannot be substituted by paraphrased GPT output because the source knowledge is in the author's lived experience and in fragmented Arabic-language hiring forums, not in indexed English content.

## What belongs in this folder

The author should write 5–10 markdown documents covering, at minimum:

1. **CV vs résumé terminology in MENA.** In Egypt, Saudi Arabia, the UAE the document is called "CV"; "résumé" is rare. Multinationals accept "Resume"; local employers expect "CV". In some Gulf jurisdictions the CV is a quasi-legal document attached to work-permit applications.

2. **Personal-information fields.** Wikipedia notes "many Middle East and African countries... require personal data (photograph, gender, marital status, children)". Document expected fields per country.

3. **Photo norms.** Formal head-and-shoulders, professional attire, neutral expression. In Saudi Arabia there are gender-specific conventions.

4. **Religion and nationality fields.** Explicit fields in some Gulf applications. Document where expected vs. unwelcome.

5. **Arabic-name romanization.** Multiple renderings (Mohamed / Mohammed / Muhammad). Document conventions, name-order (given / father / grandfather / family), and ATS-search implications.

6. **Wuzzuf, Bayt, LinkedIn MENA quirks.** Local boards have parsing quirks distinct from Greenhouse / Workday. Document Wuzzuf's English-Arabic bilingualism, Bayt's profile scoring.

7. **Salary expectations.** Stating salary on the CV is more common in MENA. Document where expected (Egypt / Gulf), where unwelcome (multinationals), and format with housing/transport/end-of-service benefits.

8. **Language fields.** Arabic / English / French (Maghreb) conventions. CEFR vs. Native/Fluent/Working/Basic.

9. **University recognition.** Cairo, AUC, KFUPM, KAUST, KSU, KSIU, GUC have different inside-vs-outside MENA profiles.

10. **Working-week conventions.** Sun–Thu in Egypt and Saudi Arabia; Mon–Fri in UAE since 2022. Hijri / Gregorian dual calendar implications.

## What does NOT belong in this folder

Do not put content here that is just paraphrased Western advice with "MENA" appended. The whole point is the *original* contribution. Facts already documented in `industry_norms/` or `ats_rules/` belong there.
