# MENA Working Week and Calendar Conventions

The MENA region does not share a single workweek convention, which has direct implications for date formats on CVs, availability statements, and notice-period clauses.

**Workweek by country (per Wikipedia's Workweek and weekend article):**

- **Sunday–Thursday workweek (Friday–Saturday weekend):** Egypt, Iraq, Libya, Sudan, Syria, Yemen, Algeria, Bahrain. Egypt: "40 hours per week, 8 hours daily."
- **Sunday–Thursday workweek (Friday–Saturday weekend, post-2013 reform):** Saudi Arabia ("Saudi Arabia switches start of weekend from Thursday to Friday" in 2013). Kuwait (since 2007), Qatar, Oman (changed 2013), Jordan (changed in the "Week of January 8, 2000"), Palestine.
- **Monday–Friday workweek (Saturday–Sunday weekend):** UAE (since 2022 — "Friday is half working day from 8 am until noon"), Lebanon, Morocco ("the standard workweek in Morocco is from Monday to Friday"), Tunisia ("Monday to Friday; 8 hours per day, 40 hours in total per week").

**Ramadan adjustments** apply across the region: Saudi Arabia reduces to "30-36 hours" weekly; Kuwait and Qatar to "25 hours"; Oman to "30 hours."

**Calendar conventions:**

- **Gregorian calendar** is the default for all CV dates region-wide.
- **Hijri (Islamic) calendar** appears on official Saudi documents but is not used on CVs. Wikipedia notes: "On 14 February 2016, Saudi Arabia adopted the Gregorian calendar for payment of the monthly salaries of government employees (as a cost cutting measure), while retaining the Islamic calendar for religious purposes."
- **Dual dating** (Gregorian + Hijri) appears on government identity documents but is unnecessary on a CV. Date formats vary: DD/MM/YYYY is dominant region-wide, MM/DD/YYYY is sometimes seen on US-template CVs adopted in Egypt.

Why this matters on a CV:

- "Available immediately" reads differently in a market with a Sun-Thu week vs. a Mon-Fri week — start dates need to align.
- A Saudi candidate with a 30-day notice period and a Mon-Fri-week target employer may need to clarify weekend conventions.
- Date ranges on the CV ("Jan 2023 – Present") should always be Gregorian and unambiguous (use month names, not numerals, to avoid DD/MM vs MM/DD ambiguity for cross-region applications).

## Concrete rule for SmartCV

Always use Gregorian dates on the CV with month names spelled out (e.g., "Jan 2023 – Present", "Sep 2020 – Jun 2024"). Never use Hijri dates on a CV, even for Saudi-targeted CVs. Never use purely numeric date formats (01/02/2024 is ambiguous between MENA DD/MM and US MM/DD). When the candidate provides an "Available from" date, assume Gregorian and present it in the same "Mon YYYY" format. Do not infer or display workweek expectations on the CV itself; surface them only in cover-letter context if the JD explicitly asks about availability.

## What public sources document

**Egypt's workweek is documented across multiple labor-law and HR sources** (accessed 2026-05-12):
- Sunday–Thursday workweek with Friday–Saturday weekend is standard (playroll.com Egypt working hours; EgyptToday labor-law report; Multiplier Egypt employment-laws guide).
- Statutory ceiling under Egyptian labor law (Article 117): "8 hours per day or 48 hours per week" excluding meals and rests (riad-riad.com overview).
- Article 120 mandates "a continuous weekly rest period of no less than 24 hours with full pay" after at most 6 consecutive working days.
- Many private-sector offices voluntarily adopt **40 hours / 5 days** within the 48-hour statutory ceiling (playroll.com; rivermate.com).
- Egypt is described as having a "usual work week" of "six consecutive days" in some sources (justlanded.com), confirming the six-day-week pattern in sectors like retail and logistics survives alongside the office five-day norm.
- Some Egyptian private companies implement a half-day Thursday schedule (multiple HR sources, accessed 2026-05-12).

**Wuzzuf's own published labor-law overview** (`wuzzuf.net/careers/working-hours-in-the-new-egyptian-labor-law-2/`, accessed 2026-05-12 — direct fetch HTTP 403, found via search result) confirms Wuzzuf treats these statutory rules as the candidate-facing reference.

**UAE's 2022 workweek change** is documented in detail (Al Jazeera, CNBC, Addleshaw Goddard, Lockton, Lexology; accessed 2026-05-12). The shift was announced in December 2021 by state news agency WAM and took effect 1 January 2022 for federal government entities. Schedule: Monday–Thursday 07:30–15:30 with Friday a half-day (07:30–12:00 in observance of Jumu'ah), and Saturday–Sunday weekend. The private sector was not legally bound but "most private sector companies were anticipated to align" with the federal change.

**Saudi Arabia's calendar move** is confirmed by Arab News and the Wikipedia Islamic Calendar article (accessed 2026-05-12): on 14 February 2016 the Saudi Cabinet adopted the Gregorian calendar "for payment of the monthly salaries of government employees (as a cost cutting measure), while retaining the Islamic calendar for religious purposes." The Umm al-Qura (Saudi civil Hijri) calendar continues to be used for legal documents and administrative purposes. CV dates region-wide are Gregorian.

**Visa Status with NOC convention** for Gulf CVs is documented (accessed 2026-05-12 via search excerpts) — "Transferable Iqama – No NOC required" and "Employment Visa – Transferable" are canonical phrases that recruiters search for. This connects to file 004's nationality/visa-status content.

I could not find a public source confirming Wuzzuf's exact date-format defaults (Mon YYYY vs numeric), nor whether Egyptian CVs ever carry a Ramadan-hours line, nor the typical "Notice Period" values used on Egyptian mid-career CVs.
