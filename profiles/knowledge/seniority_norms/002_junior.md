# Junior (0–2 years experience) Resume Conventions

A junior candidate has 0–2 years post-graduation experience — usually one full-time job, possibly with 1–2 internships. The resume sits between intern and mid-level. It demonstrates (a) shipping real code in a production team, (b) skill set matured beyond university projects, (c) clear progression direction.

**Length:** strictly one page.

**Section order:**
1. Contact info.
2. Professional summary (2–3 sentences).
3. Skills (grouped).
4. Work experience (1–2 roles, 4–5 bullets each).
5. Projects (backfills limited work history).
6. Education.
7. Optional: Certifications, Awards.

**Professional summary:** role + duration + one concrete proof point.

- BAD: "Recent CS graduate passionate about leveraging cutting-edge technology."
- GOOD: "Backend engineer, 14 months at FintechCo on the payments-reconciliation team; shipped v2 reconciliation that brought match-rate from 91% to 99.4%."

**Bullet patterns:**
- 4–5 per role, mixing feature-build with one or two process/learning bullets.
- Quantification expected, at smaller scale than mid-level.
- Avoid inflated leadership verbs. Use Contributed to / Helped ship / Owned (scoped piece) rather than Led / Spearheaded.
- Mentioning pair-programming or mentorship received is fine.

**Strong junior bullet examples:**

- "Owned end-to-end build of the customer-data export feature (FastAPI + Celery + S3 + email); shipped in 4 weeks, used by 220 customers in month 1."
- "Refactored 12 legacy Django views into the new FastAPI service in Q3; test coverage rose from 38% to 81% across migrated endpoints."
- "Contributed 8 of 22 modules in the v2 admin dashboard rewrite (React + TS); notification-settings module shipped 1 sprint ahead of estimate."
- "Investigated 4 recurring p95 spikes; root-caused 3 to connection-pool exhaustion; added monitoring now standard in the service-template."
- "Wrote the team's onboarding-doc playbook; new hires' time-to-first-PR dropped from ~10 days to 4 days."

**Junior anti-patterns:**

- Padding 2 years into 4 dense bullets with inflated language. Recruiters can tell.
- Claiming "Led" or "Architected" for IC scope. "Implemented JWT refresh-token logic" honest; "Architected a microservices auth system" at 14 months inflated.
- No metrics. Find numbers even at small scale.
- Hiding graduation year — recruiters notice the gap.

**Projects section.** Strong personal projects strengthen junior resumes. Keep 3–5 strongest from intern resume, focused on technical decisions.

**"First job" anchor.** Most-recent role bullets should clear the "can ship code in a team" bar — one on code review, one on shipping to prod, one on user benefit.

## Concrete rule for SmartCV

For junior resumes, generate one-page output with 4–5 work-experience bullets per role, mixing feature-build, refactor, investigation, and process-improvement patterns. Use Contributed to / Owned (scoped piece) / Implemented rather than Led / Spearheaded / Architected. Include at least one metric per bullet; for bullets without numerical metrics, use scope (modules, PRs reviewed, hires onboarded, runbooks authored). Keep Projects section if the candidate has strong personal-project material.
