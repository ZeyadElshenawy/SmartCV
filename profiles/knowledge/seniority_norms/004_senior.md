# Senior+ (8+ years experience) Resume Conventions

A senior+ engineer has 8+ years experience and multiple jobs where individual feature work is no longer the headline. The resume demonstrates (a) sustained impact at scope, (b) influence beyond direct deliverables, (c) judgment under uncertainty.

**Length:** two pages standard; three for principal+ with deep OSS.

**Section order:** Contact, Summary (3–4 sentences), Skills (grouped, lighter), Work experience (4–6 bullets per recent role, 2–3 for older, single-line for >12-year roles), Selected projects / OSS / publications (only if notable), Education (one line).

**Professional summary:** 3–4 sentences. Role + years + distinctive expertise + 1–2 proof points.

- BAD: "Highly accomplished senior software engineer with extensive experience."
- GOOD: "Backend engineer, 12 years; deep specialization in payment systems. Most recently led the platform-wide migration to event-sourced billing across 4 product lines and $40M annual GMV; previously owned core payment-routing through 8x traffic growth at a Series-C fintech."

**Bullet patterns:**
- 4–6 bullets per recent role demonstrating scope (multi-team, multi-quarter), architecture (trade-offs), influence (mentorship, hiring).
- Quantification shifts toward business outcomes (revenue, customer impact, org metrics).
- Each recent role needs at least one architecture bullet and one influence bullet.

**Strong senior bullet examples:**

- "Designed and led the platform-wide migration from monolithic billing to event-sourced architecture (CQRS over Kafka, Postgres snapshot store, gRPC query API); 14-month timeline, 4 squads, zero data-loss incidents during cutover."
- "Owned technical strategy for the Reliability working group across 6 teams; established the SLO framework; platform P0 incidents fell from 14 in 2023 to 4 in 2024."
- "Hired and onboarded 6 backend engineers in 2024; designed the interview rubric; first-year retention 6/6 vs. org baseline of 4/6."
- "Authored 8 RFCs in 2024 (5 approved); the approved set included Postgres logical replication, now standard across 22 services."
- "Mentored 4 mid-level engineers to senior; 3 promoted within 14 months; mentorship rubric I drafted is used by eng-management across the org."

**Senior-level anti-patterns:**

- Leading with feature-build verbs (Built, Implemented). Use Led / Designed / Owned / Drove / Mentored / Hired / Decided. Under 30% feature-build.
- Listing every technology. What differentiates is judgment about *when* to use which.
- No hiring, mentorship, or process bullets. Senior IC and management both need one.
- Older-than-12-year roles with full bullet detail. Compress to single lines.
- Self-promotion without trade-off acknowledgement reads as a red flag.

**Staff / principal.** Patterns shift toward strategy: vision docs, org-design proposals, conference talks, OSS maintainership.

## Concrete rule for SmartCV

For senior+ resumes, generate 4–6 bullets per recent role with at least one architecture bullet and one influence bullet per role. Compress roles older than 12 years to single-line summaries. Use Led / Designed / Owned / Drove / Mentored / Hired / Decided more often than Built / Implemented (target: under 30% feature-build verbs). Generate the Professional Summary as 3–4 sentences naming distinctive specialization + scope + 1–2 proof points. Drop university coursework, GPA, and most lower-context skills from the Skills section.
