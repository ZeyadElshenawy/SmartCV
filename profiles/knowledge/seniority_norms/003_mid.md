# Mid-Level (3–7 years experience) Resume Conventions

A mid-level candidate has 3–7 years of experience — typically 2–3 jobs. The resume demonstrates (a) independent end-to-end feature ownership, (b) deepening specialization or breadth-with-depth, (c) influence on team practices, mentorship, or architecture.

**Length:** one page standard; two acceptable for technical roles with deep stack + projects.

**Section order:**
1. Contact info.
2. Professional summary (2–3 sentences: role + years + 1–2 proof points).
3. Skills.
4. Work experience (3–5 bullets per recent role, 2–3 for older).
5. Education (degree, university, year; coursework dropped).
6. Optional: Projects (if notable OSS or side-project work), Certifications.

**Professional summary:** role + total years + specialization + 1–2 proof points.

- BAD: "Experienced software engineer with a passion for building scalable solutions."
- GOOD: "Backend engineer, 5 years; specialized in payment systems on Postgres + Kafka. Most recently led the migration to event-sourced billing, dropping reconciliation errors from 3% to under 0.1% for $12M/yr GMV."

**Bullet patterns:**
- 3–5 per recent role, 2–3 for older.
- Mix feature-build, architecture, performance, and influence/mentorship.
- Quantify at production / business-metric scale.
- Leadership verbs (Led, Owned, Designed) for genuinely owned projects.
- One bullet per recent role on influence beyond own code (design docs, mentorship, RFC).

**Strong mid-level bullet examples:**

- "Owned design + implementation of payment-retry (Postgres advisory locks + idempotency keys); duplicate charges fell from 0.4% to 0.01%; system processes 14K retries/day."
- "Led REST-to-gRPC migration across 8 services in Q3; median inter-service latency fell from 92ms to 38ms; deprecated 3,400 lines of REST boilerplate."
- "Authored the API design guide (consumed in every new-service review since Mar 2025); mentored 2 mid-level engineers through their first cross-team RFCs."
- "Reduced monthly AWS spend from $48K to $34K over 4 months by right-sizing 22 RDS instances and moving cold S3 data to Glacier."
- "Fixed the search-staleness bug on the backlog for 8 months; root cause was an Elasticsearch index-alias rotation race; fix has held 11 months."

**Mid-level anti-patterns:**

- Still listing coursework / GPA. Drop unless GPA is exceptional and the role rewards academic credentials.
- "Senior-style" leadership claims without scope. "Led a project" is more honest than "Led a team of N" without people-management.
- Listing every project from earlier career. The intern hackathon shouldn't still be on the resume at year 5.
- Identical-shape bullets across all roles.

**Promotion-readiness signal.** Mid-level candidates applying for senior should signal readiness through (a) 1–2 senior-shaped bullets (architecture, mentorship, cross-team influence), and (b) a Summary framing depth not years.

## Concrete rule for SmartCV

For mid-level resumes, generate 3–5 bullets per recent role and 2–3 for older roles; total 1–2 pages. Mix feature-build, architecture, performance/reliability, and influence/mentorship bullet types within each role. Drop university coursework and GPA. Generate the Professional Summary with role + years + specialization + 1–2 concrete proof points (not "experienced engineer"). Generate at least one bullet per recent role demonstrating technical influence beyond the candidate's own code (mentorship, design-doc authorship, cross-team RFC).
